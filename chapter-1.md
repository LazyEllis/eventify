## 1.1 Background to the Study

The events industry has undergone a remarkable transformation over the past decades, evolving from traditional face-to-face gatherings to sophisticated experiences that blend physical and digital elements. This evolution reflects broader changes in how people connect, communicate, and conduct business in an increasingly interconnected world. The fundamental nature of events as catalysts for networking, knowledge sharing, and community building has remained constant, even as delivery methods have evolved.

The digital transformation of the events sector has accelerated dramatically, with technology becoming integral to every aspect of event planning, execution, and evaluation. This shift has created new possibilities for event organizers while simultaneously introducing complex challenges in managing and integrating various digital tools and platforms. The integration of digital solutions has transformed everything from attendee registration to event marketing and post-event analytics.

According to Allied Market Research (2021), the global events industry has demonstrated significant economic importance, with the sector reaching a valuation of $1,135.4 billion in 2019 and projections suggesting continued growth to $1,552.9 billion by 2028 (Allied Market Research, ). This growth reflects the essential role of events in business strategy, professional development, and community building across various sectors. The industry's resilience and adaptability have been key factors in its continued expansion, despite global challenges and changing market conditions.

The COVID-19 pandemic in 2020 served as a catalyst for rapid innovation in event management, forcing organizations to quickly adapt to virtual formats and hybrid solutions. This unprecedented situation exposed both the resilience of the industry and the limitations of existing event management tools in handling complex multi-format events. The pandemic accelerated the adoption of digital solutions and highlighted the need for more flexible and integrated event management approaches.

Current event organizers face increasing pressure to deliver seamless experiences across multiple channels while managing complex logistical requirements, participant expectations, and regulatory compliance. These challenges are compounded by the need to integrate various tools and systems, often leading to inefficiencies and increased operational complexity. The demand for more sophisticated event experiences has created a need for better coordination between physical and digital elements of event delivery.

Event stakeholders - including organizers, participants, sponsors, and vendors - now demand more sophisticated solutions that can accommodate diverse event formats, provide rich engagement features, and offer comprehensive analytics while maintaining ease of use and accessibility. This has created a complex set of requirements that many existing solutions struggle to fulfill completely. The expectations for personalized experiences and meaningful engagement opportunities have risen significantly, requiring more advanced technological solutions.

While the market offers numerous point solutions for specific aspects of event management, there remains a notable gap in integrated platforms that can effectively address the full spectrum of modern event management needs while remaining accessible to organizations of various sizes and technical capabilities. The fragmentation of existing solutions creates additional complexity for event organizers, who must often juggle multiple systems and tools to deliver successful events. This fragmentation also impacts the overall efficiency and effectiveness of event management processes.

This project emerges from the need to address these challenges through the development of a comprehensive yet user-friendly event management system, aiming to bridge the gap between traditional event management approaches and the demands of contemporary event organization. By focusing on core functionality while maintaining extensibility for future enhancements, the project seeks to contribute meaningfully to the evolution of event management solutions. The goal is to create a system that not only meets current industry needs but also provides a foundation for adapting to future changes in event management practices and technologies.

## 1.3 Aim and Specific Objectives

The aim of this project is to develop a comprehensive web-based event management system (Eventify) that streamlines the process of organizing, discovering, and participating in events.

The specific objectives are to:

1. Design a model of an event management system.

2. Implement the designed model of the event management system.

3. Evaluate the developed event system.

## 1.4 Methodology

The development of Eventify will follow an Agile methodology, specifically utilizing the Scrum framework. This approach allows for iterative development, regular stakeholder feedback, and flexibility in adapting to changing requirements. The key components of the methodology include:

1. **Requirements Analysis**: Gathering and analyzing user requirements through surveys and interviews with potential users (event organizers and attendees).

2. **System Design**: Creating a detailed system architecture, database schema, and user interface designs.

3. **Iterative Development**: Implementing features in two-week sprint cycles, with regular sprint planning, review, and retrospective meetings.

4. **Testing**: Conducting unit testing, integration testing, and user acceptance testing throughout the development process.

5. **Evaluation**: Gathering user feedback and conducting performance analysis to assess the system's effectiveness and identify areas for improvement.

6. **MVP Approach**: Focusing on delivering a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) that includes core functionalities, with the potential for future enhancements.

## 1.5 Scope of the Study

This study focuses on developing a web-based event management system accessible via desktop and mobile browsers. The system will cater to various types of events, including conferences, workshops, seminars, and social gatherings, supporting both in-person and basic virtual formats. The initial implementation will target small to medium-sized events (up to 100 attendees), with a roadmap for scaling to larger events in future iterations.

The project will encompass the full software development lifecycle, from requirements gathering to system implementation, testing, and deployment. The system will be deployed on Render, a cloud hosting platform, to provide a live, accessible version of the application for demonstration and further testing purposes.

## 1.7 Definition of Terms

- **Eventify**: The name of the comprehensive event management system being developed in this project.
- **User Interface (UI)**: The graphical layout of an application through which a user interacts with the software.
- **User Experience (UX)**: The overall experience of a person using a product, especially in terms of how easy or pleasing it is to use.
- **API (Application Programming Interface)**: A set of protocols and tools for building software applications.
- **ORM (Object-Relational Mapping)**: A programming technique for converting data between incompatible type systems using object-oriented programming languages.
- **JWT (JSON Web Token)**: A compact and self-contained way for securely transmitting information between parties as a JSON object.
- **Agile**: An iterative approach to software delivery that builds software incrementally from the start of the project, instead of trying to deliver it all at once near the end.
- **MVP (Minimum Viable Product)**: A version of a product with just enough features to satisfy early customers and provide feedback for future product development.
